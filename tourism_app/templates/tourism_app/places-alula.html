Al-Ula

The World's Largest Living Museum - A Journey Through Time

Visit Al-Ula

Al-Ula is a breathtaking archaeological site in northwestern Saudi Arabia, home to magnificent rock formations, ancient tombs, and historical sites that date back thousands of years. It's often described as the world's largest open-air museum.

Hegra (Madain Saleh)

Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage site with Nabatean tombs.

Elephant Rock

A massive rock formation resembling an elephant with its trunk touching the ground.

Old Town of Al-Ula

Explore the ancient mudbrick city that was inhabited until the 20th century.

Visitor Information

Best Time to Visit

Al-Ula is best visited between October and April when temperatures are mild and perfect for exploration.

  • October-April: Ideal weather for outdoor activities
  • Winter months: Perfect for daytime exploration
  • Spring: Beautiful desert blooms
  • Summer: Very hot, limited to early morning and evening

Travel Tips

Make the most of your Al-Ula experience with these helpful tips.

  • Book guided tours in advance
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes
  • Carry water and sun protection
  • Respect archaeological sites and guidelines
  • Consider staying overnight for star gazing
